Run the content engine for a founder who is growing an audience to sell a product. The engine spans three jobs that share one spine — a hook that stops the scroll, a body that holds, a reward that earns the save or the click. The durable organic **system** decides what to post every week, **paid ad creative** buys attention from a cold stranger through one sharp lever, and **live viral research** pours a current trend into the system as fuel. One audience, one lever per piece, one clean signal per test.

Posting and every engagement action (like, follow, reply, repost, schedule, DM) are external mutations that write to a live platform. This skill researches and drafts only: stop and get the user's explicit approval before any posting or engagement action. Draft, label the draft unposted, and leave the world unchanged.

Pick the reference for the job and read it before drafting:

- [references/content-system.md](references/content-system.md) — the durable organic system: pillars, the hook-retain-reward post structure, format fit per platform, weekly cadence, repurposing, the give-before-you-sell ratio.
- [references/ad-angles.md](references/ad-angles.md) — paid ad creative: the eight-lever angle taxonomy, 3-second hook patterns, format fit, message-market-match, the test, failure modes.
- [references/viral-research.md](references/viral-research.md) — live viral research in the browser: the manual research method, the viral-pattern taxonomy, adapt-not-copy, X-versus-Instagram norms, the post-and-test loop, the ethics and ToS line.

## Steps

1. **Frame the audience, the offer, and the job.** Write the founder's niche, the one target buyer this round serves, the product the audience is grown to sell, the felt pain or desire that buyer carries, and which job runs now (organic system, paid ad creative, viral research, or a combination). For paid creative, add the awareness stage (unaware, problem-aware, solution-aware, product-aware, most-aware). This step is done when niche, buyer, product, pain-or-desire, and the named job are each written in one line.

2. **Choose the lever per piece.** For the organic system, name 3 to 5 content pillars at the intersection of founder expertise, buyer pain, and product adjacency. For paid creative, pick 3 to 5 distinct angles from the eight-lever taxonomy (pain, desire, curiosity, social proof, authority, contrarian, us-vs-them, transformation). For viral research, scan the live browser per the ethics line and capture 8 to 12 spiking posts, then extract each to its transferable pattern. This step is done when the job's unit exists: 3 to 5 pillars each with an audience payoff, or 3 to 5 angles each naming a different lever, or at least 3 captured posts each reduced to a one-line pattern stripped of its subject.

3. **Write the hook and the body on the shared spine.** Draft each piece as hook, retain, reward (organic and viral) or as a 3-second hook plus a beat-by-beat script or slide sequence (paid), using a named hook pattern. Reject any opening line that states a topic instead of a tension, a stake, a contradiction, or a promise. This step is done when each piece carries a distinct hook line that would stop a target stranger on frame one with no sound and no brand name, plus a retain body and an explicit reward or call-to-action.

4. **Fit the format to the platform.** Assign each piece one platform-native format from the format-fit tables: X favors the standalone post and the thread, Instagram favors the carousel, the Reel, and the story, and paid creative adds the static image and the UGC creator-style format. The format reinforces the lever rather than fighting it. This step is done when each piece names one platform and one format, and that format reinforces its lead lever.

5. **Plan cadence or the test.** For the organic system, place drafts on a 7-day calendar at a sustainable frequency with pillars rotated, then confirm the give-before-you-sell ratio holds at roughly four value pieces to one ask. For paid creative, set the constant (one audience), the varied variable (the angle), the budget split, and the win metric (3-second hold rate or click-through). For viral research, propose slots on top of the founder's baseline calendar with a success signal and a double-down threshold per draft. This step is done when each draft carries a slot or a test role, and the ratio threshold or the win metric is stated.

6. **Repurpose one idea across formats.** Take one strong insight and recut it into platform-native pieces — one X thread, one IG carousel, one Reel script, one story prompt — rebuilding the hook and pacing per room rather than cross-posting identical text. This step is done when one source insight has spawned at least three distinct platform-native pieces, none a copy-paste of another.

7. **Screen against the failure modes and the consent line.** Check the drafted set against the failure-modes and red-flags lists in the reference for the job (clever-but-unclear, no hook, single-angle testing, ignoring the platform, all-promo, off-brand trend-chasing, copying instead of adapting, vanity virality, ToS-risky automation). Confirm the drafts are labeled unposted and no engagement action was taken. This step is done when the set trips zero red flags, and posting plus engagement actions stay pending the user's explicit approval.
